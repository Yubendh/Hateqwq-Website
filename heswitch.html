Days' Personal Hall Effect Switch Roundup

By Days

Introduction

The hall effect switch market is evolving at a rapid pace. New options seemingly pop up every month, each offering different typing feels and sound signatures from their competitors. As someone deeply invested in the mechanical keyboard community, I've been following hall effect keyboards closely, always chasing after that switch that truly replicates the satisfying mechanical keyboard feel while offering benefits of what hall effect provides.

The main challenge with hall effect switches is trying to balance the performance aspect of HE switches will trying to maintain the satisfying sound & feel that matches mechanicals.

Testing Methodology

Keyboard: Venom60HE
Case: Frog Mini Leggera
Mounting: 45A hardness o-ring
Plate: Aluminum plate
Keycaps: Keykobo Black on white
Sound Test Keys: G, H, J and Space bar

All switches have been put through hours upon hours of constant gaming and typing to see how the switch performs overtime and if there are any changes in consistency and lube dispersion.

The Switches

Geonworks Raw HE

This offering from Geonworks have been among the first switches to truly chase and push the limits of how hall effect can sound or feel. At 0.5$/switch, nowadays this is considered a nice mid-priced switch that offers a nice high pitched sound with a soft bottom out that doesn't feel too fatiguing.

The main issues that make this switch lose their popularity among the hall effect community is their stem wobble, inconsistent lube job, and unpleasant bottom out sound & ticking from the switch.

Specifications
Housing Material:
PC top, Nylon bottom
Spring Weight:
57g bottom out
Travel Distance:
3.8mm
Factory Lube:
Light factory lubrication
Purchase

Duhuk Core HE

Duhuk Core HE are a newer offering that garnered alot of hype especially from Geon who's used it in a build stream. The special shtick with these switches is that they have a unique dampening on the top out of the switch. Meaning you'll mostly only hear your switch bottoming out.

The sound signature on the bottom out is a bit more deeper and muted than the Raws but overall sounds much cleaner and less chattery. This can be due to the the amounts of factory lube in the switch, which can actually leak onto the plate leaving a small ring of lube. Some people may dislike the dampened top out sound of the Duhuk Cores as it feels very unsatisfying and almost topping out on a piece of rubber or silicone, although what they've done is basically add a dampening mechanism to the stem of the switch only.

Specifications
Housing Material:
POM top and bottom
Spring Weight:
55g bottom out
Travel Distance:
3.6mm
Factory Lube:
Medium-heavy factory lubrication
Purchase

TTC King of Magnets (KOMs)

The most hyped up switch for performance snobs. Said to be one of the most stable switches for high perfoming keyboards. A box stem design to ensure minimal stem wobble and stability for low mm rapid trigger and actuation.

Despite all that, these have to be one of the worst sounding and feeling switches I've used on a hall effect keyboard. Not only is the bottom out incredibly harsh, both the bottom and top out feels and sounds incredibly plasticky and high pitched. On top of their price of 1$/switch and the absolutely terrible factory lubing, this is seriously a switch I cannot recommend in the 2025.

To give an idea on how bad the factory lubing is, a normal switch would have lube on contact points to... reduce contact & friction. Yeah KOM's do absolutely none of that, the factory lube is blobbed on the bottom of the housing where there is absolutely 0 contact and TTC decided to use incredibly thick lube that is not suitable for lubing these parts of the switch.

The Wuque Studio Flux switches offer a premium experience with an incredibly smooth keystroke and satisfying bottom out. Their sound signature is quite balanced, neither too high nor too low pitched, making them versatile for various keyboard builds.

Closing Thoughts

After extensively testing these hall effect switches, I've found that the magnetic switch market has truly matured, offering serious competition to traditional mechanical switches. The unique combination of smooth, contactless actuation with satisfying sound profiles has won me over as a mechanical keyboard enthusiast.

What stands out most is how each switch in this lineup brings something unique to the table. The Raw HE offers premium refinement, the Duhuk Core provides a deeper sound signature, TTC KOMs bring consistency, and the Wuque Flux balances all aspects wonderfully. My frankenswitch experiment also demonstrates the exciting customization possibilities in the hall effect space.

For those concerned about durability, these magnetic switches promise significantly longer lifespans than traditional mechanical switches due to their contactless operation. The ability to adjust actuation points (on compatible PCBs) is another major advantage that makes these switches versatile for both typing and gaming.

My Recommendations

For typing enthusiasts: The Raw HE and the Frankenswitch combination offer the most satisfying typing experience with their excellent balance of sound and feel.

For gamers: TTC KOMs with their consistent actuation and snappy return are ideal for competitive gaming.

Best all-rounder: Wuque Flux strikes an excellent balance between typing and gaming performance.

Best value: Duhuk Core HE offers a premium experience at a more accessible price point.

My personal favorite: After all my testing, I've settled on the Frankenswitch (Duhuk housing + Raw HE stem) as my daily driver. The combination of the smooth POM housing with the premium stem creates a typing experience that's truly unmatched.

As the hall effect switch market continues to evolve, I'm excited to see what innovations come next. For now, these switches have convinced me that magnetic switches can indeed replicate and even enhance the mechanical keyboard experience I've always loved.
